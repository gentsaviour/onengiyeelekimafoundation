ONENGIYE ELEKIMA FOUNDATION

ONENGIYE ELEKIMA FOUNDATION is a financial service provider specialized in payday loans for individuals and corporate organizations. Established in 2016, we are committed to bridging short-term financial gaps for employees of corporate entities, ensuring seamless cash flow and financial security.

The Onengiye Elekima Foundation (OEF) is a nonprofit organization dedicated to empowering communities, eradicating poverty, and uniting Nigerian youth through sports and education. Since 2016, we’ve worked with donors and local partners to deliver impactful projects in underserved areas, providing free education, healthcare awareness, and financial support to families in need.

Our Mission

We are committed to promoting unity and peace among Nigerian youths by using the transformative power of sports. Through the Unity Cup 2025, an under-17 grassroots football competition, we provide a platform for young people to connect, compete, and grow together.

Our Vision

We envision a Nigeria where sports break down barriers and inspire a generation of empowered, united, and peace-loving youth. Initiatives like the Unity Cup foster mutual respect, social cohesion, and national pride.

Unity Cup Objectives

  • Foster unity and peace across diverse communities.
  • Promote teamwork, inclusion, and mutual respect.
  • Empower youth to become ambassadors of change.
  • Encourage cultural exchange and social justice.
  • Provide equal opportunities for boys and girls in sports.
  • Build sustainable programs with lasting impact.

The Unity Cup is more than a tournament — it’s a movement for change. By investing in young talent and promoting equality through sports, we’re building a better Nigeria. Join us in making a difference.

Lives Saved

Helpers Social Development Foundation is a project to eradicate the root cause of poverty in African countries starting from Nigeria. Helpers Foundation has placed itself as one of the best foundations helping poor families in Nigeria. Your donation will impact locally-led projects and our local partner’s nonprofit organizations.

Nigeria is a country of over 206 million out of which, 133 million Nigerians are poor. About 38% of out-of-school children in Nigeria are girls. 20 million children of school-going age are out of school in Nigeria. 60.1% of the population in Nigeria lives in poverty. Nigeria’s unemployment rate stands at 33%. We believe that this number should not exist in Nigeria and other African countries, and that is why we are working hard with our partners to end it.

The Nigerian population is growing every day and unfortunately, the government no longer provides for the needs of its citizens. There are growing challenges in our country hence parents are no longer able to properly take care of their children. We strongly believe that everyone should have access to sound education, quality healthcare delivery, and economic empowerment. We are driving positive change through tree planting and climate change education in public schools with children in Nigeria. We emphasize the critical importance of focusing climate action and the acquisition of green schools in Nigeria. Your support will go a long way to achieving our goals in Nigeria and other African countries.
